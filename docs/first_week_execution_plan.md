# First-Week Execution Plan

## Week-one goal

Validate whether local service business owners will pay for CiteLocal AI without ad spend, paid tools, or product build-out.

Success target by end of day 7:

- 50 qualified prospects sourced.
- 10 free snapshots prepared.
- 30 personalized outreach messages sent manually.
- 5 positive replies or snapshot requests.
- 2 discovery calls booked.
- 1 paid audit or implementation sprint sold.

If no sale happens, the fallback success signal is at least 5 meaningful replies that reveal whether the niche, message, or offer needs adjustment.

## Operating constraints

- Zero budget.
- No paid APIs.
- No automated sending.
- No spam blasts.
- No dependence on prior ventures or warm audiences.
- No ranking, lead, or AI citation guarantees.
- Manual, specific, founder-led outreach only.

## Tools to use

Free tools only:

- Browser/manual search.
- Google Search and Maps manually.
- Business websites.
- Public social profiles.
- Spreadsheet or CSV.
- CiteLocal local audit/report generator.
- Email account and contact forms manually.
- Calendar link if already free; otherwise offer 2–3 time windows manually.
- Markdown/PDF export using local/browser tools if available.

## Day 1: Pick niche and define the list

### Outcome

One vertical, one metro, and first 20 leads captured.

### Tasks

1. Choose vertical and metro.
   - Default: HVAC in one midsize metro.
2. Create prospect spreadsheet/CSV.
3. Search core service terms.
4. Add 20 prospects.
5. Score each 0–10 using the qualification score.
6. Write 5 common observed gaps.

### Metrics

- 20 prospects added.
- At least 10 prospects score 7+.
- At least 5 have clear owner/contact path.

### End-of-day decision

If fewer than 10 qualified prospects are found, broaden the geography or switch to plumbing/roofing.

## Day 2: Complete first 50 prospects

### Outcome

50 total prospects, scored and segmented.

### Tasks

1. Add 30 more prospects from directories, map results, trade associations, and local content searches.
2. Identify owner/manager/contact path where possible.
3. Check each site for 1–3 visible gaps.
4. Rank prospects by fit.
5. Select top 10 for snapshots.

### Metrics

- 50 prospects total.
- Top 10 selected.
- At least 30 have email/contact form.
- At least 20 have a specific personalization note.

## Day 3: Create first 5 snapshots and send 10 messages

### Outcome

First personalized outreach is live.

### Tasks

1. Run or manually prepare 5 snapshots for top prospects.
2. Draft one-line personalization for 10 prospects.
3. Send 10 manual first-touch messages.
4. Log every message.
5. Prepare reply snippets for “send it,” “cost,” and “already have SEO.”

### Metrics

- 5 snapshots complete.
- 10 messages sent.
- 0 generic templates sent without personalization.

### Quality bar

Each message must include:

- Business name.
- Service/category.
- City/area.
- One concrete observed gap.
- Low-friction CTA.

## Day 4: Create 5 more snapshots and send 10 messages

### Outcome

10 snapshots ready and 20 total messages sent.

### Tasks

1. Prepare snapshots for prospects 6–10.
2. Send 10 more first-touch messages.
3. Respond to any replies within the same day.
4. Offer discovery calls to positive replies.
5. Tighten the top 3 value points based on reply language.

### Metrics

- 10 snapshots complete.
- 20 total messages sent.
- First replies logged.

## Day 5: Follow up and push to calls

### Outcome

30 total messages sent and follow-up cadence started.

### Tasks

1. Send first follow-up to day-3 prospects who did not reply.
2. Send 10 more first-touch messages.
3. For interested prospects, send snapshot summary inline.
4. Ask for call or offer paid audit.
5. Track objections verbatim.

### Metrics

- 30 first-touch messages sent.
- 10 follow-ups sent.
- At least 2 call asks made.

## Day 6: Discovery calls and offer refinement

### Outcome

Convert interest into paid audit/sprint or learn why not.

### Tasks

1. Hold booked discovery calls.
2. Use the discovery script.
3. Offer $149 audit first if prospect is cautious.
4. Offer $399 sprint if prospect wants done-for-you help.
5. Send payment/onboarding message immediately after verbal yes.
6. If calls are not booked, send a “top 3 fixes” email to warm replies and ask for a 15-minute walkthrough.

### Metrics

- 1–2 calls held or scheduled.
- At least one direct paid offer made.
- All objections logged.

## Day 7: Review, decide, and prepare next batch

### Outcome

Clear decision: continue niche, adjust message, or pivot vertical.

### Tasks

1. Count total leads, messages, replies, calls, and sales.
2. Review which personalization angles got replies.
3. Identify best lead source.
4. Update scripts with winning language.
5. Decide next 50 prospects.
6. If a client paid, prioritize delivery before more outreach.

### Metrics review

Track:

- Prospects sourced.
- Qualified prospects.
- Snapshots created.
- Messages sent.
- Positive replies.
- Negative replies.
- Discovery calls booked.
- Paid audits sold.
- Fix sprints sold.
- Main objections.

## Decision rules

### Continue same niche if:

- Positive reply rate is 10%+.
- At least 2 calls are booked.
- Owners understand the pain after explanation.
- Gaps are common and easy to identify.

### Change message if:

- Prospects reply but misunderstand the offer.
- They think it is generic SEO.
- They ask “what is AI visibility?” often.

Fix by leading with customer questions and website clarity, not “AI.”

### Change niche if:

- Fewer than 20 qualified prospects are findable.
- Contact paths are poor.
- Businesses are mostly chains/franchises.
- Owners do not care about website-driven calls.

Next-best verticals:

1. Plumbing.
2. Roofing.
3. Pest control.
4. Electrical.
5. Med spa only after tightening compliance language.

## Daily operating rhythm

### Morning: source and audit

- Add prospects.
- Review websites.
- Prepare snapshots.

### Midday: outreach

- Send manual emails/forms/DMs.
- Keep messages short.
- Log status immediately.

### Afternoon: replies and calls

- Respond to warm replies.
- Book calls.
- Send snapshot summaries.

### End of day: improve

- Record what worked.
- Rewrite one script line.
- Pick next day’s top 10.

By day 7, maintain:

- `docs/lead_sourcing_zero_budget.md`
- `docs/cold_email_dm_scripts.md`
- `docs/discovery_call_and_onboarding.md`

- `data/outreach_log.csv` if actively operating.

## Recommended founder focus

Do not build more software until one of these happens:

- One paid customer.
- Five strong positive replies.
- Two prospects ask for the same report improvement.

Until then, the highest-leverage work is manual prospecting, sharper snapshots, and calls with real local operators.
