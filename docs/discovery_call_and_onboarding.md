# Discovery Call Script and Onboarding Checklist

## Discovery call goal

Convert interested prospects into one of two paid next steps:

- **$149 Full AI Answer Visibility Audit**
- **$399 AI Visibility Fix Sprint**

The call should confirm fit, identify the most valuable service/customer segment, explain the audit in plain language, and close a small paid engagement.

## Pre-call prep

Before the call, collect:

- Business name and website.
- Primary services.
- Main city/service area.
- Google Business Profile link if available.
- Review count/rating if visible.
- 3 observed visibility gaps.
- 1 likely high-value service to focus on.
- Contact name and role.

Prepare a 3-bullet snapshot:

1. What is already strong.
2. What is unclear for buyers/AI answers.
3. What to fix first.

## 20-minute discovery call agenda

### 0:00–2:00 — Set the frame

“Thanks for taking the time. I’ll keep this practical. I reviewed the site through the lens of whether customers and AI-style search answers can quickly understand what you do, where you do it, and why they should trust you. I’ll ask a few questions, share what I noticed, and if it looks useful I can explain the audit/fix options.”

### 2:00–7:00 — Understand business priorities

Ask:

- “What services are you most trying to grow right now?”
- “Which jobs are most valuable: repairs, installs, maintenance plans, emergency calls, or something else?”
- “What cities/neighborhoods matter most?”
- “How do most new customers find you today?”
- “Are you happy with the quality of calls from your website?”
- “Do you already have someone handling SEO or website updates?”

Listen for:

- High-margin service focus.
- Seasonal urgency.
- Existing marketing spend frustration.
- Easy implementation path.

### 7:00–11:00 — Diagnose answer-readiness gaps

Ask:

- “What questions do customers ask before booking?”
- “Do customers ask about price ranges, financing, response time, warranties, or service area?”
- “Do you publish answers to those questions on the site?”
- “Can someone update the website if we provide copy/schema recommendations?”
- “What proof matters most to customers — reviews, license, years in business, family-owned, photos, guarantees?”

### 11:00–15:00 — Share snapshot findings

Use this structure:

“Here’s what I noticed.”

- “Strong point: {{positive_observation}}.”
- “Gap 1: {{specific_gap}}. This matters because {{business_reason}}.”
- “Gap 2: {{specific_gap}}. This matters because {{business_reason}}.”
- “Gap 3: {{specific_gap}}. This matters because {{business_reason}}.”

Then say:

“The pattern is not that your site is bad. It’s that it could be much more explicit about the facts that buyers and AI summaries look for: services, locations, costs, FAQs, trust proof, and structured business details.”

### 15:00–18:00 — Present offer

Option A:

“The lower-friction next step is the $149 audit. I’ll deliver the full report, priority fixes, and a 30-day content plan within 48 hours. You can hand it to whoever updates the site.”

Option B:

“If you want the first fixes done instead of just a report, the $399 sprint includes the audit plus draft FAQ answers, service-area copy, trust-proof blocks, and schema your site person can publish. If you want me to publish directly, I can do that with access and approval.”

### 18:00–20:00 — Close

Use one of these:

- “Which option is more useful: the $149 plan or the $399 fix sprint?”
- “Do you want to start with the audit and decide on implementation after?”
- “If I can deliver this by {{date}}, are you comfortable starting with the audit today?”

If yes:

“Great. I’ll send a simple invoice/payment link and onboarding questions. Once those are in, I’ll start the audit.”

If not now:

“No problem. What would need to be true for this to be worth doing?”

## Objection handling

### “We already do SEO.”

“That’s good. This is narrower than SEO. I’m checking whether your pages directly answer the questions AI summaries and high-intent customers need answered: service area, costs, response time, trust proof, schema, and FAQs. The deliverable can become a punch list for your SEO person.”

### “Can you guarantee results?”

“No — and I would not trust anyone who guarantees rankings or AI citations. What I can guarantee is a concrete list of publishable improvements. If the audit does not include at least 10 specific fixes, I’ll refund it.”

### “We do not have budget.”

“Understood. The cheapest useful option is the $149 audit, and you can implement it gradually. If now is not the right time, I can still send the free snapshot and you can use the quick wins internally.”

### “Our website vendor handles this.”

“Perfect. The audit is written so your vendor can implement it. I can also keep the recommendations limited to copy, schema, FAQs, and page structure so it does not become a redesign project.”

### “AI search is not important yet.”

“That may be true for some customers. The reason I focus on it now is that the same fixes also help regular customers: clearer service pages, better FAQs, stronger trust proof, and structured local business details.”

## Paid onboarding checklist

### Required client inputs

Collect these before starting:

- Business legal/display name.
- Website URL.
- Primary contact name and email.
- Best phone number.
- Primary city and full service area.
- Top 3 services to grow.
- Highest-value service/job type.
- Emergency/same-day availability details.
- License number or license/insurance wording, if applicable.
- Warranty/guarantee details.
- Years in business.
- Review/testimonial sources.
- Brand voice preference: plain, premium, family-owned, technical, fast/emergency, etc.
- Competitors they respect or want to beat.
- Website platform and who can publish changes.

### Access checklist for implementation sprint

Only request what is needed:

- CMS login or collaborator invite, if client wants direct publishing.
- Google Business Profile access is optional; do not require at launch.
- Existing brand assets/photos if adding trust proof sections.
- Approval contact for copy changes.
- Backup/export confirmation if editing directly.

### Delivery checklist: $149 audit

Deliver:

- Audit report as HTML/PDF or shareable file.
- Executive summary.
- Score/grade with explanation.
- Top 10 prioritized fixes.
- 30-day content plan.
- Suggested FAQ questions and answers outline.
- Schema recommendations.
- Trust-signal recommendations.
- 30-minute review call or async Loom-style walkthrough if available without paid tools.

### Delivery checklist: $399 sprint

Deliver everything in the audit plus:

- Updated copy for one priority service page.
- 8–12 FAQ answers.
- Service-area copy block.
- Trust proof copy block.
- JSON-LD schema draft.
- Page title/meta suggestions.
- Publishing notes for client/webmaster.
- Before/after summary.
