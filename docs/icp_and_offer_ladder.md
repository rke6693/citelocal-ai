# CiteLocal AI ICP and Offer Ladder

## Launch positioning

**CiteLocal AI helps owner-led local service businesses become easier for AI answer engines and Google-style AI results to understand, trust, and recommend.**

The launch offer is not “AI SEO magic.” It is a practical visibility audit and fix plan that improves the clarity of the business website: service areas, services, FAQs, schema, trust proof, and direct answers buyers search for before calling.

## Best launch niche

### Primary ICP: owner-operated HVAC companies in one metro

Start with **HVAC companies in one midsize metro** such as Cincinnati, Columbus, Indianapolis, Louisville, Nashville, Charlotte, Tampa, Phoenix suburbs, or another market the founder can manually research.

Why HVAC first:

- High customer value: emergency repair, replacement, maintenance plans.
- Clear high-intent queries: “AC repair near me,” “furnace replacement cost,” “emergency HVAC [city].”
- Buyers ask question-heavy searches that AI systems summarize.
- Many local HVAC sites have thin service-area pages, weak FAQs, missing schema, or generic copy.
- Owners understand missed calls and seasonal demand.
- Easier to show concrete fixes without needing paid data.

### Ideal prospect profile

Prioritize businesses with:

- 1–3 locations, not a franchise or national chain.
- 10–200 Google reviews, enough credibility but not enterprise-level marketing maturity.
- Public website with service pages but weak buyer-answer content.
- Clear phone number and contact form.
- Owner, founder, GM, or marketing manager visible on site, LinkedIn, Facebook, or local directories.
- Services worth at least hundreds to thousands of dollars per job.
- Obvious gaps: no FAQ page, no cost/pricing page, no service-area clarity, no LocalBusiness/HVACBusiness schema, missing license/insurance/warranty proof, generic title tags.

### Avoid at launch

- National chains and large franchises with agency gatekeepers.
- Businesses with no website or only a Facebook page.
- Sites fully controlled by an existing SEO agency where changes require politics.
- Medical/legal verticals until claim language and compliance review are tighter.
- Restaurants/retail where AI answer visibility is less directly tied to high-ticket calls.

## Buyer personas

### Owner/operator

- Cares about: more quality calls, lower dependency on ads, local reputation.
- Language to use: “Your website should answer the questions customers ask before calling.”
- Avoid: jargon like “LLM optimization,” “vector embeddings,” or ranking guarantees.

### Office manager / GM

- Cares about: fewer repetitive questions, cleaner service pages, easier customer education.
- Language to use: “We can make your site clearer so searchers and AI summaries understand exactly what you do, where, and why they should trust you.”

### Marketing coordinator

- Cares about: practical to-do list, content calendar, schema, measurable website improvements.
- Language to use: “You’ll get a prioritized fix list and copy blocks your team can publish.”

## Core pain statement

Most local service websites were written for old-school SEO pages, not answer engines. They list services, but they often fail to clearly answer:

- Who exactly serves this city?
- What services are available now?
- What does it cost or what affects cost?
- Why should a customer trust this company?
- Is the company licensed, insured, reviewed, and locally present?
- What makes the company quotable in an AI answer?

CiteLocal AI turns those gaps into a simple report and implementation plan.

## Offer ladder

### 0. Free AI Visibility Snapshot

**Price:** Free  
**Purpose:** Start conversations and prove relevance.  
**Delivery time:** 10–15 minutes per prospect once workflow is practiced.

Includes:

- 1-page scorecard or short email.
- Top 3 visibility gaps.
- 1 example query the business should be eligible to appear in.
- 1 quick-win recommendation.
- Soft CTA to book a 15-minute call or buy the full audit.

Positioning:

> “I checked whether your site is easy for AI answer engines to understand for HVAC searches in {{city}}. I found a few quick fixes that could make your services and service area clearer.”

### 1. Full AI Answer Visibility Audit

**Price:** $149 launch price  
**Delivery:** 48 hours  
**Best for:** Owners who want a plan before implementation.

Deliverables:

- Full HTML/PDF audit report.
- Priority issue list ranked by impact and ease.
- Service-area clarity review.
- FAQ/cost-answer recommendations.
- Trust signal checklist.
- Schema/structured data recommendations.
- 30-day content plan with weekly tasks.
- 30-minute review call.

Acceptance criteria:

- Owner knows exactly what to update first.
- At least 5 publishable page/FAQ improvements are included.
- No ranking, citation, or lead guarantees are made.

### 2. AI Visibility Fix Sprint

**Price:** $399 launch price  
**Delivery:** 5 business days after receiving access/content approvals  
**Best for:** Owners who want fixes done for them.

Includes:

- Everything in the $149 audit.
- Rewrite or improve one priority service page.
- Add or draft 8–12 FAQ answers.
- Draft LocalBusiness/HVACBusiness schema JSON-LD.
- Draft one service-area page or section.
- Add trust-proof copy blocks: licensing, warranty, reviews, years in business, emergency availability.
- Before/after summary.

Access options:

- Preferred: deliver copy and schema for their webmaster to publish.
- Optional: implement directly if they provide CMS access and approval.

### 3. Monthly AI Visibility Maintenance

**Price:** $49/month launch price  
**Delivery:** Monthly  
**Best for:** Simple recurring revenue after audit/fix sprint.

Includes:

- Monthly re-scan.
- One new FAQ/content brief.
- One service-area or seasonal content suggestion.
- Review of changed pages.
- Short monthly action email.

Upsell path:

- If the client wants publishing done monthly, offer a separate $199/month “done-for-you content update” add-on after the first few customers.

## Launch guarantee / risk reversal

Use a narrow, controllable promise:

> “If the audit does not include at least 10 specific fixes your team can publish, I’ll refund it.”

Avoid:

- “Guaranteed rankings.”
- “Guaranteed ChatGPT citations.”
- “Guaranteed leads.”
- Claims that imply direct control over Google, OpenAI, Perplexity, Bing, or any third-party system.

## First niche recommendation

For the first 7 days, pick **one vertical + one metro** only.

Recommended default:

- Vertical: HVAC.
- Metro: the founder’s local/known city or a midsize city with many independent contractors.
- Prospect count: 50.
- Outreach target: 30 highly personalized messages in week one.
- Goal: 5 replies, 2 discovery calls, 1 paid audit or fix sprint.
